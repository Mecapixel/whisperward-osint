# WhisperWard OSINT — Training Data Sources

**Version:** 4.0 | **Last Updated:** June 2026 | **Maintainer:** Pixora Inc.

This document records all sources used to develop the grooming pattern
classifier and risk scoring heuristics in WhisperWard OSINT.

---

## Classifier Pattern Sources

All patterns in `behavioral_classifier.py` are derived from the following
public domain sources only. No private law enforcement databases, no real
victim communications, and no proprietary datasets were used.

### Primary Sources

**NCMEC — National Center for Missing and Exploited Children**
Online enticement research and published documentation on predator
communication patterns. Available at missingkids.org. Used for:
isolation language patterns, secrecy solicitation patterns, and
gift/incentive offer patterns.

**Thorn — Defenders of Children**
Technical research reports on predator behavior, platform migration
patterns, and digital grooming methodology. Available at thorn.org.
Used for: platform migration pressure patterns, age probing patterns,
and multi-step sequence definitions.

**Internet Watch Foundation (IWF)**
Published behavioral documentation on online child sexual exploitation
methodology. Available at iwf.org.uk. Used for: trust building patterns
and compliment escalation patterns.

**Federal ICAC Prosecution Records**
Public court documents from Internet Crimes Against Children Task Force
prosecutions available through PACER (pacer.gov) and published case
summaries. Used for: identity probing patterns and gift incentive patterns.

---

## Synthetic Data Sources

All test and validation data is generated by `synthetic_profile_generator.py`.
The fabricated chat messages in that module were written by Pixora Inc.
developers to reflect documented grooming patterns without reproducing
real communications.

No real children's accounts, no real predator accounts, and no real
platform data were used in development, testing, or validation at any stage.

---

## What Was Explicitly Not Used

The following sources were considered and explicitly excluded:

- Real chat logs of any kind, regardless of origin or authorization status
- Private law enforcement databases or task force intelligence
- Social media scrapes or platform data exports
- Dark web content of any kind
- Any content depicting or describing real victims
